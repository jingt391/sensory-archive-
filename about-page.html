Memories often carry the weight of people's deep-seated past.

 

Each object may seem ordinary and insignificant, but it is through people that meaning and memory are bestowed upon them. Objects are not merely physical entities; they hold within them stories and emotions.

 

 

When we touch or see a particular item, it often triggers a recollection of a specific moment, like a window opening to the past.

 

 

As time passes, memories gradually fade, and the details become unclear. Yet, the emotions and impressions remain deeply rooted within us.

 

 

It is through the intertwining of objects and memories that we feel the passage of time, experiencing the sedimentation and transformation of the years.
